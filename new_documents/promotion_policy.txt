TechNova Solutions - Employee Handbook
Document: Promotion and Career Advancement Policy
Last Updated: December 2024
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Promotion Policy Overview

1.1 Philosophy
At TechNova Solutions, we believe in developing and promoting talent from within. Our promotion process is designed to be fair, transparent, and based on demonstrated performance and readiness for increased responsibility.

1.2 Commitment to Growth
TechNova is committed to:
- Providing clear paths for career advancement
- Recognizing and rewarding strong performance
- Developing team members for future roles
- Making promotion decisions based on merit
- Ensuring equity in advancement opportunities

2. Types of Career Advancement

2.1 In-Role Promotion
Advancement within your current job family:
- Moving from Associate to mid-level to Senior
- Increasing scope and responsibility
- Based on demonstrated readiness for next level
- Does not require applying for a new position

2.2 Internal Mobility
Moving to a different role or team:
- Lateral moves to new functions
- Promotions to roles in different departments
- Requires applying through internal job board
- Standard interview process applies

2.3 Management Track
Transitioning to people management:
- Moving from individual contributor to manager
- Requires demonstrated leadership capabilities
- Must complete manager readiness assessment
- Includes additional training requirements

3. Promotion Criteria

3.1 Performance Expectations
To be considered for promotion, team members must:
- Consistently meet or exceed performance expectations
- Demonstrate behaviors aligned with TechNova values
- Show readiness to perform at the next level
- Have positive feedback from stakeholders
- No active performance improvement plans

3.2 Level-Specific Competencies
Each level has defined competencies:

Individual Contributors:
- Technical/functional expertise
- Problem-solving and decision-making
- Collaboration and communication
- Initiative and ownership
- Business impact

Managers:
- All IC competencies plus:
- People development
- Team leadership
- Strategic thinking
- Resource management
- Change leadership

3.3 Time in Role
While there is no strict requirement, we generally expect:
- Minimum 12 months in current role before promotion
- Demonstrated sustained performance (not just recent spike)
- Exceptions considered for exceptional performers

4. Promotion Process

4.1 Promotion Cycles
TechNova conducts promotions twice per year:
- Q1 Cycle: Effective April 1 (planning in January-February)
- Q3 Cycle: Effective October 1 (planning in July-August)

4.2 Process Timeline
Each promotion cycle follows this timeline:

Planning Phase (Month 1):
- Managers identify potential promotion candidates
- Initial discussions with team members
- Preliminary business justification developed

Documentation Phase (Month 2):
- Promotion documents prepared
- Stakeholder feedback collected
- Compensation recommendations developed

Calibration Phase (Month 3):
- Department calibration sessions
- Cross-functional review for senior roles
- Final decisions made

Communication Phase:
- Approvals finalized
- Team members notified
- Promotions announced

4.3 Promotion Documentation
Promotion cases must include:

Business Justification:
- Why the role at next level is needed
- Business impact of the team member
- Scope expansion or increased responsibility

Performance Evidence:
- Key accomplishments in current role
- Examples of next-level work already demonstrated
- Stakeholder feedback and endorsements

Values Alignment:
- How team member exemplifies TechNova values
- Examples of cultural contribution

Development Plan:
- Areas for continued growth at new level
- Support and resources needed

5. Calibration Process

5.1 Purpose
Calibration ensures:
- Consistent standards across teams
- Fair evaluation of all candidates
- Appropriate leveling across departments
- Equity in promotion decisions

5.2 Calibration Sessions
Calibration involves:
- Department leadership reviews all candidates
- Comparison against level expectations
- Discussion of borderline cases
- Final recommendations made

5.3 Participants
Calibration sessions include:
- Department head (decision maker)
- Skip-level managers
- HR Business Partner
- Peer managers (for perspective)

6. Compensation Changes

6.1 Promotional Increases
Promotions typically include:
- Salary increase to bring within new level range
- Target range: midpoint of new salary band
- Minimum increase: 10% (or market adjustment if higher)
- Equity refresh grant consideration

6.2 Salary Band Placement
New salary determined by:
- New role salary band
- Current salary relative to new band
- External market data
- Internal equity considerations
- Budget availability

6.3 Effective Date
Compensation changes are effective:
- First of the month following approval
- Retroactive adjustments not provided
- Reflected in next regular payroll

7. Off-Cycle Promotions

7.1 When Considered
Off-cycle promotions may be considered for:
- Significant scope change mid-cycle
- Retention of critical talent
- Merger of roles or responsibilities
- Exceptional circumstances

7.2 Approval Process
Off-cycle promotions require:
- Department head approval
- HR review and endorsement
- VP or C-level approval
- Must meet all standard promotion criteria

7.3 Documentation
Off-cycle promotions require:
- Full promotion documentation
- Justification for off-cycle timing
- Compensation recommendation
- Skip-level endorsement

8. Management Promotions

8.1 Becoming a Manager
Transitioning to management requires:
- Demonstrated leadership capabilities
- Interest in developing others
- Willingness to prioritize team success
- Completion of manager readiness assessment

8.2 Manager Readiness Assessment
Assessment includes:
- Leadership competency evaluation
- 360-degree feedback review
- Scenario-based discussions
- Commitment to management responsibilities

8.3 New Manager Requirements
Newly promoted managers must:
- Complete New Manager Essentials training (first 90 days)
- Partner with HR Business Partner
- Develop team development plans
- Conduct regular 1:1s with direct reports

9. Senior Leadership Promotions

9.1 Director and Above
Senior promotions (Director+) require:
- Executive calibration review
- Cross-functional endorsement
- Extended performance track record
- Strategic impact demonstration

9.2 Additional Considerations
Senior promotions evaluate:
- Organizational leadership
- Business strategy contribution
- External market presence
- Succession and talent development

10. When Promotions Are Declined

10.1 Reasons for Declining
Promotions may be declined due to:
- Insufficient evidence of next-level readiness
- Business need not established
- Recent performance concerns
- Calibration reveals stronger candidates

10.2 Feedback and Next Steps
If promotion is declined:
- Manager provides clear, specific feedback
- Development areas identified
- Timeline for re-evaluation discussed
- Support and resources offered

10.3 Re-Evaluation
Team members may be reconsidered:
- Next promotion cycle (typical)
- Off-cycle if significant progress demonstrated
- Continuous development encouraged

11. Equity and Fairness

11.1 Equal Opportunity
TechNova ensures:
- Promotion decisions based on merit
- No discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Regular analysis of promotion rates by demographic
- Interventions if disparities identified

11.2 Transparency
We maintain transparency through:
- Published promotion criteria and process
- Clear level expectations in job frameworks
- Regular communication about promotion cycles
- Access to promotion data (aggregate)

11.3 Addressing Concerns
If you have concerns about promotion equity:
- Discuss with your manager
- Escalate to HR Business Partner
- Use anonymous feedback channels
- Contact DEI team
