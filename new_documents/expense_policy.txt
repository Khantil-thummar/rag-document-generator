1. Travel and Expense Policy

1.1 Policy Purpose
TechNova Solutions' Travel and Expense Policy provides guidance to all team members to ensure responsible spending of company resources. This policy aims to:
- Provide clear guidelines for reimbursable business expenses
- Ensure efficient use of company funds
- Maintain transparency in business-related spending
- Prevent misuse of company resources
- Comply with tax and legal requirements

1.2 General Principles
All team members are expected to:
- Exercise good judgment when incurring business expenses
- Spend company money as if it were their own
- Choose cost-effective options without sacrificing quality or safety
- Submit accurate and timely expense reports
- Retain all receipts for expenses over $25

1.3 Expense Submission Process

1.3.1 Expense Reporting System
All expenses must be submitted through Expensify, our expense management platform.

1.3.2 Submission Requirements
- Submit expenses within 30 days of incurring them
- Include itemized receipts for all expenses over $25
- Provide clear descriptions explaining the business purpose
- Categorize expenses correctly
- Tag expenses to appropriate projects or cost centers

1.3.3 Approval Workflow
- Expenses under $500: Manager approval required
- Expenses $500-$2,000: Manager and Finance approval required
- Expenses over $2,000: VP approval required
- All international travel: Pre-approval required from department head

1.4 Travel Expenses

1.4.1 Air Travel
- Book flights at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Economy class is standard for flights under 6 hours
- Premium economy permitted for flights over 6 hours
- Business class requires VP approval and is limited to flights over 8 hours
- Use the company's preferred booking platform (TravelPerk) for best rates
- Personal frequent flyer miles may be kept by the traveler

1.4.2 Ground Transportation
- Rental cars: Intermediate class or below; book through TravelPerk
- Rideshare (Uber/Lyft): Permitted for business travel; avoid premium services
- Personal vehicle: Reimbursed at IRS standard mileage rate (currently $0.67/mile)
- Public transit: Fully reimbursable with receipts
- Parking: Reimbursable at airports and business destinations

1.4.3 Lodging
- Maximum nightly rate: $250 in standard markets, $350 in high-cost cities
- High-cost cities include: New York, San Francisco, London, Tokyo, Singapore
- Book through TravelPerk for corporate rates
- Incidentals (mini-bar, movies) are not reimbursable
- Room cancellations must be made 24 hours in advance to avoid charges

1.4.4 Meals During Travel
Daily meal per diem rates:
- Domestic travel: $75 per day
- International travel: $100 per day
- Per diem covers breakfast, lunch, dinner, and tips
- Alcohol is not covered by per diem and must be expensed separately (see 1.5)

1.5 Client Entertainment and Meals

1.5.1 Client Meals
- Reasonable meal expenses with clients are reimbursable
- Maximum per person: $100 for dinner, $50 for lunch, $30 for breakfast
- Must document attendees and business purpose
- Pre-approval required for groups larger than 6 people

1.5.2 Alcohol Policy
- Moderate alcohol with client meals is permitted
- Maximum 2 drinks per person
- Alcohol at internal team events requires department head approval
- Never expense alcohol consumed alone

1.5.3 Entertainment
- Client entertainment (sporting events, shows) requires pre-approval
- Maximum $200 per person per event
- Must have clear business justification
- Gift cards for clients are not permitted as expenses

1.6 Remote Work Expenses

1.6.1 Home Office Equipment
One-time home office setup stipend: $1,000
Eligible expenses include:
- Desk and ergonomic chair
- Monitor and accessories
- Keyboard, mouse, webcam
- Lighting equipment
- Noise-canceling headphones

1.6.2 Ongoing Remote Work Expenses
Monthly reimbursable expenses:
- Internet: Up to $75/month
- Phone plan (if used for work): Up to $50/month
- Co-working space: Up to $300/month

1.7 Professional Development

1.7.1 Training and Conferences
- Annual professional development budget: $2,000 per team member
- Conferences require manager approval
- Registration, travel, and lodging covered within budget
- Unused budget does not roll over to next year

1.7.2 Books and Subscriptions
- Work-related books: Fully reimbursable
- Professional subscriptions: Reimbursable with manager approval
- Online courses: Covered under professional development budget

1.8 Office Supplies and Equipment

1.8.1 Office Supplies
- Standard office supplies: Order through company procurement portal
- Emergency purchases: Reimbursable up to $50 without pre-approval
- Larger purchases require procurement process

1.8.2 Software and Tools
- All software must be requested through IT
- Personal software subscriptions require manager and IT approval
- SaaS tools must go through security review before purchase

1.9 Team Events and Celebrations

1.9.1 Team Meals
- Team lunch/dinner budget: $50 per person
- Quarterly team events: $100 per person
- Manager approval required for all team events
- Include list of attendees in expense report

1.9.2 Celebrations
- Birthday celebrations: $25 per person limit
- Work anniversaries: $50 per person limit
- Baby showers/weddings: Coordinate with HR for company contribution

1.10 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following are NOT reimbursable:
- Personal travel or vacation expenses
- Traffic tickets, parking violations, or legal fines
- Personal grooming or clothing (unless safety equipment)
- Gym memberships or personal fitness
- Child care or pet care during travel
- Spouse/partner travel expenses
- Political or religious contributions
- Personal credit card interest or late fees
- Lost or stolen personal items

1.11 Expense Report Deadlines
- Submit all expenses within 30 days of incurring them
- Quarter-end deadline: All expenses must be submitted by the 5th of the following month
- Late submissions may be rejected or require additional approval
- End-of-year deadline: December 31st expenses due by January 10th

1.12 Receipt Requirements
Receipts must show:
- Vendor name and address
- Date of purchase
- Itemized list of goods/services
- Total amount including tax
- Payment method

Acceptable receipt formats:
- Original paper receipts (photographed or scanned)
- Digital receipts forwarded to receipts@expensify.com
- Credit card statements are NOT acceptable as sole documentation

1.13 Currency and Exchange Rates
- Expenses in foreign currency will be converted at the exchange rate on the date of expense
- Use company credit card for international expenses when possible
- Document exchange rate if paying with personal funds

1.14 Compliance and Auditing
- All expense reports are subject to random auditing
- Fraudulent expense claims will result in disciplinary action, up to termination
- Repeated policy violations may result in loss of expense privileges
- Questions about policy should be directed to finance@technova.com

1.15 Contact Information
- Expense Support: expenses@technova.com
- Travel Booking: travel@technova.com
- Finance Questions: finance@technova.com
- Policy Exceptions: Submit request through HR portal
