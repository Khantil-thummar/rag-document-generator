Document: Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Policy
Last Updated: December 2024
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion at TechNova

1.1 Our Commitment
TechNova Solutions is committed to building and maintaining a diverse, equitable, and inclusive workplace where every team member can thrive. We believe that diverse perspectives drive innovation, enhance decision-making, and create better products for our customers.

1.2 DEI Vision
Our vision is to create a workplace where:
- Every team member feels they belong and are valued
- Diverse perspectives are actively sought and embraced
- Equitable opportunities exist for all
- Inclusive practices are embedded in everything we do
- Our workforce reflects the diversity of the communities we serve

2. Definitions

2.1 Diversity
Diversity refers to the range of human differences, including but not limited to:
- Race and ethnicity
- Gender and gender identity
- Sexual orientation
- Age and generation
- Disability status
- Religion and belief
- National origin
- Socioeconomic background
- Education and experience
- Veteran status
- Thinking styles and perspectives

2.2 Equity
Equity means ensuring fair treatment, access, and opportunity for all team members by:
- Identifying and removing barriers to participation
- Providing resources based on individual needs
- Addressing systemic disadvantages
- Creating level playing fields for success

2.3 Inclusion
Inclusion is the practice of creating an environment where:
- All team members feel welcome and respected
- Diverse voices are heard and valued
- Everyone can participate fully and authentically
- Differences are celebrated, not merely tolerated

2.4 Belonging
Belonging is the outcome of successful DEI efforts, where team members:
- Feel accepted for who they are
- Experience psychological safety
- Can bring their whole selves to work
- Feel connected to colleagues and the organization

3. DEI Strategic Pillars

3.1 Pillar 1: Diverse Workforce
Building a workforce that reflects diverse communities:
- Inclusive hiring practices and diverse candidate slates
- Partnerships with diverse professional organizations
- University recruiting at HBCUs, HSIs, and other diverse institutions
- Internship programs targeting underrepresented groups
- Removing bias from job descriptions and requirements

3.2 Pillar 2: Equitable Systems
Creating fair processes and policies:
- Regular pay equity analyses and adjustments
- Transparent promotion criteria and processes
- Equitable access to stretch assignments and visibility
- Standardized performance evaluation criteria
- Accessible workplace accommodations

3.3 Pillar 3: Inclusive Culture
Fostering a culture where everyone belongs:
- Inclusive leadership development programs
- Unconscious bias training for all team members
- Employee Resource Groups for community building
- Celebrating diverse holidays and cultural events
- Safe channels for raising concerns

4. Employee Resource Groups (ERGs)

4.1 About ERGs
Employee Resource Groups are voluntary, employee-led groups that:
- Foster connection among team members with shared identities
- Provide professional development and mentorship
- Advise on company policies and practices
- Support recruiting and retention efforts
- Create educational opportunities for allies

4.2 Current ERGs at TechNova
- TechNova Women: Supporting women and allies
- TechNova Pride: LGBTQ+ community and allies
- TechNova Black Excellence: Black employees and allies
- TechNova Unidos: Latino/Hispanic employees and allies
- TechNova Abilities: Team members with disabilities and allies
- TechNova Veterans: Military veterans and families
- TechNova API: Asian and Pacific Islander employees and allies
- TechNova Parents: Working parents and caregivers
- TechNova Next Gen: Early-career professionals

4.3 ERG Support
TechNova supports ERGs through:
- Annual budget for events and activities
- Executive sponsors from leadership team
- Paid time for ERG activities (up to 4 hours/month)
- Platform for ERG visibility and communication
- ERG leader recognition and development

4.4 Starting an ERG
To propose a new ERG:
- Submit proposal to dei@technova.com
- Gather at least 10 interested team members
- Identify leadership team and executive sponsor
- Develop mission statement and goals
- Present to DEI Council for approval

5. Hiring and Talent Practices

5.1 Inclusive Hiring
Our hiring practices include:
- Diverse interview panels for all positions
- Structured interviews with consistent questions
- Skills-based assessments over credential requirements
- Blind resume review where feasible
- Inclusive job descriptions using gender-neutral language

5.2 Diverse Candidate Slates
For all positions:
- Aim for diverse candidate slates at interview stage
- Partner with diverse recruiting sources
- Track diversity metrics through hiring funnel
- Provide training for hiring managers on inclusive hiring

5.3 Retention and Development
Retaining diverse talent through:
- Mentorship programs pairing diverse talent with leaders
- Sponsorship initiatives for high-potential diverse employees
- Leadership development programs with diverse cohorts
- Regular engagement surveys with demographic analysis
- Stay interviews to understand retention drivers

6. Pay Equity

6.1 Commitment to Pay Equity
TechNova is committed to equal pay for equal work:
- Annual pay equity analysis across all demographics
- Adjustments made to address unexplained disparities
- Transparent salary bands for all positions
- No salary history questions in hiring

6.2 Pay Transparency
We practice pay transparency by:
- Publishing salary ranges in job postings
- Sharing salary band information with team members
- Explaining compensation philosophy and decisions
- Providing clear criteria for advancement and raises

7. Accessibility and Accommodations

7.1 Physical Accessibility
TechNova offices are designed for accessibility:
- Wheelchair accessible entrances and restrooms
- Accessible parking and transportation options
- Adjustable desks and ergonomic equipment
- Quiet spaces for those who need them

7.2 Digital Accessibility
Our digital products and tools meet accessibility standards:
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance for internal tools
- Screen reader compatible systems
- Captioning for video content
- Accessible document formats

7.3 Requesting Accommodations
Team members needing accommodations should:
- Contact HR at accommodations@technova.com
- Engage in interactive process to determine needs
- Receive reasonable accommodations in timely manner
- Know that accommodation requests are confidential

8. Inclusive Culture Practices

8.1 Inclusive Meetings
We practice inclusive meeting norms:
- Rotate meeting times for global teams
- Provide agendas in advance
- Create space for all voices to be heard
- Use inclusive language
- Record meetings for those who cannot attend
- Respect different communication styles

8.2 Inclusive Language
We use inclusive language by:
- Using gender-neutral terms (team members, not guys)
- Respecting chosen names and pronouns
- Avoiding idioms that may not translate across cultures
- Being mindful of ableist language
- Updating documentation to reflect inclusive terms

8.3 Pronouns
We support pronoun sharing by:
- Encouraging (not requiring) pronoun sharing
- Adding pronouns to email signatures and Slack profiles
- Training on proper pronoun usage
- Correcting mistakes gracefully

9. Reporting and Accountability

9.1 Reporting Concerns
If you experience or witness discrimination or exclusion:
- Report to your manager (if comfortable)
- Contact HR at hr@technova.com
- Use the anonymous ethics hotline
- Reach out to any DEI Council member
- All reports are investigated and kept confidential

9.2 Non-Retaliation
TechNova prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports DEI concerns in good faith
- Participates in investigations
- Advocates for DEI initiatives
- Provides feedback on inclusive practices

9.3 Accountability
Leaders are accountable for DEI through:
- DEI goals in performance evaluations
- Regular reporting on team diversity metrics
- Participation in DEI training and initiatives
- Modeling inclusive behaviors

10. Training and Education

10.1 Required Training
All team members complete:
- Unconscious Bias Fundamentals (upon hire)
- Inclusive Behaviors workshop (annual)
- Harassment Prevention (annual)

10.2 Additional Learning
Optional learning opportunities include:
- Allyship in Action workshop
- Inclusive Leadership for managers
- Cultural competency sessions
- ERG-hosted educational events

11. Measuring Progress

11.1 Metrics We Track
We measure DEI progress through:
- Workforce diversity at all levels
- Hiring and promotion rates by demographic
- Retention rates by demographic
- Pay equity analysis results
- Engagement survey results by demographic
- ERG participation rates

11.2 Transparency
We share DEI data through:
- Annual diversity report (public)
- Quarterly updates to all team members
- Board-level DEI reporting
- External benchmarking
