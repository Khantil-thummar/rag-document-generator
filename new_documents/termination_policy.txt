TechNova Solutions - Employee Handbook
Document: Offboarding and Termination Policy
Last Updated: December 2024
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Offboarding and Termination Policy

1.1 Overview
This policy outlines the procedures for team member departures from TechNova Solutions, whether voluntary or involuntary. Our goal is to ensure a smooth transition for departing team members while protecting company interests.

1.2 Offboarding Philosophy
TechNova strives to make every departure respectful and professional. We aim to:
- Treat departing team members with dignity
- Ensure proper knowledge transfer
- Maintain positive relationships with alumni
- Protect company assets and information
- Comply with all legal requirements

2. Voluntary Terminations (Resignations)

2.1 Resignation Notice
Team members who choose to leave TechNova should:
- Provide written notice to their manager
- Standard notice period is 2 weeks
- Manager and director roles: 4 weeks notice recommended
- Submit resignation through Workday

2.2 Resignation Process
Steps for resigning team members:
1. Speak with your manager before submitting formal resignation
2. Submit resignation in Workday with your last day of work
3. Work with manager on transition and handoff plan
4. Complete exit checklist provided by HR
5. Participate in exit interview (optional but encouraged)
6. Return all company property

2.3 Notice Period Expectations
During your notice period:
- Continue to fulfill job responsibilities professionally
- Document ongoing projects and processes
- Train replacements or team members on your duties
- Complete knowledge transfer sessions
- Tie up loose ends on current projects

2.4 Manager Responsibilities
When a team member resigns, managers should:
- Accept the resignation professionally
- Discuss transition timeline and priorities
- Create a handoff plan
- Communicate departure to stakeholders (with employee input)
- Ensure access is revoked on the last day
- Complete manager offboarding checklist

3. Involuntary Terminations

3.1 Types of Involuntary Termination
Involuntary terminations may occur due to:
- Performance issues after documented coaching
- Policy or conduct violations
- Position elimination or restructuring
- End of contract or temporary assignment
- Failure to meet job requirements

3.2 Performance-Based Termination
Before termination for performance:
- Team member placed on Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- Clear expectations and timeline documented
- Regular check-ins during improvement period
- Final decision made by manager, HR, and leadership
- Documentation maintained throughout process

3.3 Termination for Cause
Immediate termination may occur for:
- Gross misconduct or violence
- Theft or fraud
- Harassment or discrimination
- Violation of confidentiality agreements
- Illegal activity
- Falsification of records

3.4 Position Elimination
When positions are eliminated:
- Affected team members notified as soon as possible
- Severance provided according to company policy
- Outplacement support offered
- COBRA and benefits information provided
- References provided for future employment

4. Offboarding Process

4.1 Offboarding Checklist
HR initiates offboarding which includes:

Before Last Day:
- Final paycheck calculation prepared
- Benefits continuation information prepared
- IT notified for access termination
- Equipment return arranged
- Exit interview scheduled (if desired)

On Last Day:
- All system access revoked
- Badge and physical access deactivated
- Final walkthrough with manager
- Equipment collected
- Personal items collected

4.2 Knowledge Transfer
Departing team members should:
- Document all ongoing projects and their status
- Provide passwords and access information (via secure channels)
- Introduce replacement to key contacts
- Create handoff documentation
- Complete final status updates

4.3 Company Property Return
Team members must return:
- Laptop and charger
- Mobile devices and accessories
- Office keys and access badges
- Credit cards and expense accounts
- Physical documents and files
- Any other company property

For remote workers:
- Prepaid shipping label provided for equipment
- Equipment must be shipped within 5 business days
- Failure to return may result in payroll deduction

5. Final Pay and Benefits

5.1 Final Paycheck
Final pay includes:
- All wages through last day of work
- Accrued but unused vacation (where required by law)
- Any outstanding expense reimbursements
- Commission or bonus per applicable plans

Timing of final pay:
- Voluntary resignation: Next regular payday
- Involuntary termination: Within 72 hours or as required by state law
- California employees: Final pay on last day of work

5.2 Benefits Continuation
Upon termination:
- Health insurance: Coverage ends last day of month
- COBRA: Information mailed within 14 days
- 401(k): Account remains yours; options provided
- Life insurance: Conversion options available
- FSA/HSA: Spending deadline information provided

5.3 Severance

Eligibility:
Severance may be provided for:
- Position eliminations (layoffs)
- Restructuring-related terminations
- At company discretion for other separations

Severance is generally NOT provided for:
- Voluntary resignations
- Termination for cause or misconduct
- Performance-based terminations

Severance Package (when applicable):
- 1 week of pay per year of service (minimum 2 weeks)
- COBRA subsidy for equivalent period
- Outplacement services
- Requires signing separation agreement

6. Exit Interviews

6.1 Purpose
Exit interviews help TechNova:
- Understand reasons for departure
- Identify areas for improvement
- Gather feedback on management and culture
- Maintain positive alumni relationships

6.2 Process
Exit interviews are:
- Voluntary but strongly encouraged
- Conducted by HR (not departing team member's manager)
- Confidential and constructive
- Used for aggregate trend analysis
- Scheduled during the final week

6.3 Topics Covered
Exit interviews may discuss:
- Reasons for leaving
- Job satisfaction and challenges
- Management and team dynamics
- Company culture and values
- Suggestions for improvement
- Likelihood of recommending TechNova

7. System Access Termination

7.1 Access Revocation Timeline
Access is revoked according to this schedule:
- Email and Slack: End of last working day
- Code repositories: End of last working day
- Customer systems: Before last working day
- Building access: End of last working day
- VPN and internal systems: End of last working day

7.2 Data Handling
Before departure:
- Personal files may be exported from company drives
- Work files must remain accessible to team
- Email forwarding set up if needed
- Customer contacts transitioned to new owner

8. Rehire Eligibility

8.1 Eligibility Status
Departing team members are marked as:
- Eligible for rehire: Left in good standing
- Not eligible for rehire: Termination for cause
- Eligible with conditions: Case-by-case review needed

8.2 Rehire Process
Former team members who wish to return:
- Apply through standard hiring process
- Previous manager may be consulted
- Previous tenure does not guarantee rehire
- If rehired, treated as new employee for benefits

9. References

9.1 Reference Policy
TechNova provides employment verification including:
- Dates of employment
- Position(s) held
- Rehire eligibility status

Additional references:
- Managers may provide personal references
- Personal references are voluntary
- Must use personal contact information, not company
- Cannot share confidential HR information

10. Communication of Departure

10.1 Internal Communication
Departures are communicated:
- Team: Manager informs direct team members
- Department: Department head sends broader communication
- Company: Significant departures may warrant company-wide notice
- Timing coordinated with departing team member

10.2 External Communication
For customer-facing roles:
- Customer contacts transitioned before departure
- Messaging coordinated with departing team member
- New point of contact clearly communicated
- No disparagement of departing team member

11. Special Circumstances

11.1 Death of Team Member
In case of team member death:
- Benefits paid to designated beneficiaries
- Final pay to estate or designated person
- Personal effects returned to family
- Memorial tribute coordinated with family input

11.2 Retirement
For team members retiring:
- Celebration/recognition event offered
- Extended transition period when possible
- Consulting arrangement may be offered
- Alumni network access provided
